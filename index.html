SYMPHONNY Exhibition

Firstly, our group aims to create a free space of audio for participants to be both producer( sender) and listener(receiver) while they are experiencing. They will operate five exhibited phones with different playlists. It can be seen as an exploratory journey or an experience. We will guide participants to produce sound in unison as a symphony which has four movements from low to high pitches:

1. an opening sonata or Allegro

2. a slow movement, such as adagio

3. a minute or scherzo with trio

4. an allegro, rondo, or sonata

Specifically, weâ€™d like to organize our work and conduct our participants to follow these four stages above to produce a real symphony instead of some random sound combinations. For instance, we will open up to two phones with low-pitch ambient sound to be exhibited at the beginning.Then we will increase the number of the exhibited phone by 1 (with higher-pitch sound) every 10 minutes until the 5th phone joins in. We will take the ambient sound (two phones of stage1) off when the 5th phone join in (which is the last movement of the symphony). One complete round will take 40 minutes. During the 40 minutes, we are going to conduct participants to create a symphony in four movements.

What the film is about...

My project is called "Open Your Eyes." In this 30 s short video, I will show a lot of discontinuity editing, but to some extent they are linked.
